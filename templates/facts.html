Psoriasis

Facts

  • Psoriasis is a chronic autoimmune condition.
  • It often results in red, itchy, and scaly patches on the skin.
  • Trigger factors include stress, infections, and certain medications.

Remedies

  • Moisturize regularly
  • Use medicated creams as prescribed.
  • Manage stress through relaxation techniques.

Seborrheic Dermatitis

Facts

  • Commonly known as dandruff when it affects the scalp.
  • Causes red, greasy skin covered with flaky white or yellow scales.
  • Linked to an overgrowth of a yeast called Malassezia.

Remedies

  • Use medicated shampoos
  • Avoid harsh soaps and detergents.
  • Apply anti-fungal creams as directed.

Lichen Planus

Facts

  • An inflammatory condition that affects the skin, mucous membranes, and nails.
  • Characterized by purplish, flat-topped bumps.
  • The exact cause is often unknown.

Remedies

  • Topical corticosteroids for skin lesions.
  • Antihistamines for itching.
  • Avoid triggers like certain medications.

Pityriasis Rosea

Facts

  • Common skin rash that usually begins with a single, large patch.
  • Followed by the appearance of smaller lesions in a "Christmas tree" pattern.
  • Believed to be caused by a viral infection.

Remedies

  • Over-the-counter antihistamines for itching.
  • UV light exposure may help.
  • Keep skin moisturized.

Chronic Dermatitis

Facts

  • Inflammation of the skin that persists over a long time.
  • Itchy, red skin that may thicken over time.
  • Multiple causes, including genetics and environmental factors.

Remedies

  • Identify and avoid triggers.
  • Topical corticosteroids for inflammation.
  • keep skin hydrated

Pityriasis Rubra Pilaris

Facts

  • Rare skin disorder characterized by orange-red scaling plaques and keratotic follicular papules.
  • Often starts on the head and neck and may spread to the trunk and limbs.
  • The cause is unknown, but it is believed to involve genetic factors.

Remedies

  • Treatment may involve topical steroids.
  • Emollients to soothe dry skin.
  • Consultation with a dermatologist for specialized care.
